# LLC Setup — Skeel Software LLC (Connecticut)

- **NAICS Code:** 513210 (Software Publishers)
- **Filed:** 2026-03-05
- **Status:** Approved

## Formation

| Item | Cost | Where | Status |
|------|------|-------|--------|
| Certificate of Organization | $120 | [business.ct.gov](https://business.ct.gov/business-services/business-forms-and-fees) | Done |
| EIN (Employer Identification Number) | Free | [IRS online](https://sa.www4.irs.gov/applyein/) | Done |

### Steps

1. File Certificate of Organization online at business.ct.gov ($120)
2. Get an EIN from the IRS (free, instant, online)
3. Open a business bank account (free at most banks with EIN)
4. Update App Store / Play Store publisher to the LLC name
5. Keep business and personal finances separate

## Ongoing Obligations

### Annual Report — $80/year

- **What it is:** A one-page form confirming your LLC's basic info (name, address, registered agent, members). No financials or revenue reporting.
- **Filing window:** January 1 – March 31 every year
- **Where:** business.ct.gov
- **Consequence of not filing:** Late fees, eventual dissolution of the LLC
- **Reminder:** CT may or may not send a reminder. Set a recurring calendar reminder for early January.

### Taxes

Single-member LLC is a **disregarded entity** — the IRS ignores it. All income/expenses flow through to your personal 1040 via Schedule C.

**If the app makes no money:**
- No extra tax obligations
- Business expenses (Apple developer fee, hosting, etc.) are deductible against other personal income on Schedule C

**If the app makes a small amount (<$10-15K/year):**
- Report on Schedule C (personal tax return)
- Self-employment tax: 15.3% on net profit (Social Security + Medicare)
- Normal income tax rate on that profit
- Quarterly estimated tax payments to IRS and CT if you expect to owe $1,000+/year

**If it starts making real money:**
- Same as above, numbers get bigger — get a CPA
- Consider S-corp election to reduce self-employment tax (good problem to have)

**CT-specific:**
- No franchise tax on LLCs
- No business entity tax for single-member LLCs
- Pass-Through Entity Tax does NOT apply to single-member LLCs
- No state-level business tax return — income is reported on your personal CT return

## Liability Protection

The LLC separates personal and business liability. To maintain this protection:

- **Do** keep a separate business bank account
- **Do** file the annual report every year
- **Don't** pay personal expenses from the business account (commingling funds)
- **Don't** let the LLC lapse by missing annual filings
