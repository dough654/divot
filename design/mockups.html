Cadence
Track-listing energy meets warm metallics. Home screen is numbered strips — like a setlist or workout routine. Camera viewfinder bleeds edge-to-edge with a floating translucent control bar. Clips shown as a horizontal filmstrip. Settings stripped bare — no cards, just text and lines. Warm amber on black velvet.
Syne + Manrope Warm black / Amber / Cream Numbered strips Edge-to-edge viewfinder Filmstrip clips No-card settings

Terrain
Outdoor brand energy — sage greens, terracotta accents, earth tones on dark loam. Home screen breaks into an asymmetric tile grid (camera gets a tall tile, viewer and clips share right column). Camera and viewer are full-bleed with gradient fade controls. Clips as a thumbnail grid. Think Patagonia meets TrackMan.

Zero
Stripped to nothing. Pure typography — no icons, no cards, no decoration. Home screen is three massive words stacked vertically. Camera is a bare rectangle with monospace labels. Clips are just text. Everything communicated through scale, weight, and negative space. Two colors: black and white. One accent: electric blue. Like if Helmut Lang designed a golf app.
Darker Grotesque + Space Mono Pure B&W + electric blue No icons No cards Typography only Monospace data

Stark
Zero's typographic confidence married to Cadence's usable structure. Massive Darker Grotesque numbers anchor each strip — you know what's tappable. Monospace data labels give the camera HUD genuine personality without sacrificing legibility. Filmstrip clips stay scannable. Settings are bare text with real toggle controls. Pure B&W with one electric blue accent — no decoration that doesn't earn its place.
Darker Grotesque + Space Mono Pure B&W + electric blue Massive numbered strips Monospace HUD camera Filmstrip + numbered list No-card settings
